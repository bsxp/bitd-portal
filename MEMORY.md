# Blades in the Dark - Document Reference

This directory contains reference PDFs from the **Blades in the Dark** tabletop RPG by John Harper. The game is set in Doskvol, a haunted industrial city where players run a crew of daring scoundrels building a criminal enterprise.

## Files

### Core Rules

- **BitD_ruleDetails.pdf** (32 pages) — The core system rules. Covers the conversation-based play structure, dice mechanics (d6 pool, read highest), the four roll types (Action, Downtime, Fortune, Resistance), position & effect, and the game's four-phase loop: Free Play > Score > Downtime > repeat. The primary mechanical reference.

- **BitD_coin.pdf** (2 pages) — Coin & Stash rules. Monetary values (1-10 coin scale), coin use (extra downtime activities, boosting rolls, avoiding entanglements, advancing Tier), stash mechanics for retirement, and crew vault storage limits.

- **BitD_factions.pdf** (4 pages) — The Faction Game. Tier system (0-V, measuring wealth/influence/scale), Hold (strong vs. weak), Rep mechanics (earn 12 to advance), faction status levels (+3 Allies to -3 War), and the Claims system (seizing territory from rival factions via the crew's claim map).

### Crew & Characters

- **BitD_crew.pdf** (33 pages) — Crew creation and crew type details. Covers choosing a crew type (Assassins, Bravos, Cult, Hawkers, Shadows, Smugglers), selecting reputation & lair, establishing hunting grounds, and the full crew sheets with special abilities, upgrades, and claim maps for each type.

- **BitD_playerCrewAdvancement.pdf** (2 pages) — PC and Crew advancement rules. XP triggers (desperate rolls, playbook-specific triggers, beliefs/drives/heritage, vice/trauma struggles), crew XP triggers, filling advancement tracks to earn new special abilities or action dots, and rules for changing playbooks or crew type.

- **blades_sheets_v8_2_Cutter.pdf** (1 page) — The Cutter character playbook sheet. A dangerous, intimidating fighter. Lists special abilities (Battleborn, Bodyguard, Ghost Fighter, Leader, Mule, Not to be Trifled With, Savage, Vigorous, Veteran), action ratings, items, friends, and XP trigger ("address a challenge with violence or coercion").

### Operations

- **BitD_score.pdf** (11 pages) — Running a Score (the heist/operation phase). Planning & engagement mechanics: six plan types (Assault, Deception, Stealth, Occult, Social, Transport), the engagement roll (fortune roll setting starting position), flashbacks, teamwork actions, and extended examples of play.

- **BitD_downtime.pdf** (15 pages) — Downtime phase rules. Covers payoff (Rep + Coin earned from scores), Heat & Wanted Level, entanglements, and downtime activities: Vice (stress relief + overindulgence), stash, acquiring assets, long-term projects, recovering from harm, training, and reducing Heat.

### Setting

- **BitD_doskvol.pdf** (2 pages) — Map and overview of Doskvol. City map with all districts (Barrowcleft, Brightstone, Charhollow, Charterhall, Coalridge, Crow's Foot, The Docks, Dunslough, Nightmarket, Silkshore, Six Towers, Whitecrown) and landmarks (The Void Sea, The Lost District, Ironhook Prison, Gaddoc Rail Station, Eeleries & Farms, Old North Port, The Deathlands).

### Quick Reference

- **blades_core_playsheets.pdf** (3 pages) — Player and GM quick-reference sheets. Scoundrel moves (pursue goals, perform daring actions, resistance, flashbacks), GM moves (introduce threats, tick clocks, devil's bargains), threat lists by category (charged, strange, security, tricky, social, violent), and action roll outcome tables for Controlled/Risky/Desperate positions.
